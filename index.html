Data Exploration in SQL:COVID-19

In this Project, I use SQL to analyze and answer questions about Covid 19 Data.

Data Summary:

The dataset contains valuable information related to COVID-19 deaths and vaccinations between the years of January 2022 to January 2023. The data has key information based off of region, date, total deaths, new deaths, total vaccinations, and new vaccinations. This dataset is useful to track trends and answer questions about the impact that COVID- 19 and the COVID-19 vaccines have had on a regional and global level.

Questions:

1.What is the likelihood of dying if you contract covid in your country?

2. What percentage of the population is infected with Covid?

3. What countries have the highest Infection Rate compared to the Population?

4. What are the countries with the Highest Death Count per Population?

5. What are the continents with the highest death count per population?

6. What do the global numbers look like?

7. What percentage of the Population has received at least one Covid Vaccine?

Skills Performed:

1.Select the Data I would be working with.

2.Use various SQL commands like SELECT, FROM, WHERE, GROUP BY, ORDER BY, and JOIN to explore the data set.

3.Use Aggregate Functions to perform calculations on the data sets and convert data types to retrieve the correct calculations.

4.Create a CTE and Temp Table to perform calculations on the Partition By in the previous query.

5.Creta a View to store data for later visualization.

Excel Dashboard:Bike Sales

In this Project, I use Excel to analyze and create a dashboard on a companies Bike Sales data.

Data Summary:

This dataset shows who purchased a bike based on different demographics. The different demographics are based of off the following: marital status, occupation, education, income, childre, gender, home owners, cars owned, region, commute distance, and age. This information is useful for companies that sell bikes because based of off the findings they can decide who their target audience is. It can also help companies further discuss and investigate on ways they can increase their sales towards a demographic that may not be their current target audience.

Skills Performed:

1. Clean the raw data to make it usable for analyzation.

2. Create Pivot Tables to select data for Dashboard.

3. Create dashboard to display findings of the anlyzation. And add slicers to the dashboard so that users can filter data for themselves based on different demographics.

Findings:

1. Based of off customer commute, those with a shorter commute distance tend to purchase bikes more in comparison to those with a longer commute.

2.Based on average income and gender, more Males purchase bikes.

3.Middle Aged customers, who are 31-54, tend to buy bikes more in comparison to adolescents and seniors.
